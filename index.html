TERRY A. DAVIS

1969 – 2018

Alone, over roughly a decade, he wrote an operating system from the ground up — kernel, compiler, language, editor, and graphics library — targeting a machine he described as a "temple" for God. This is a tribute to the engineering, not the ending.

02 HolyC, briefly

HolyC was Terry's own language: C syntax fused with a built-in interpreter, so code could run instantly or compile to machine code with no separate build step. Numeric literals could be typed straight into range-checked integer types, and the shell itself was just HolyC. Here's a flavor of it. 

U0 Main()
{
  I64 i;
  "Hail the Lord!\n";
  for (i = 0; i < 8; i++)
    Print("Line %d of 640x480\n", i);
}
Main;

U0 = "void". Functions could be called directly at the prompt, like a live REPL over the whole OS.

04 Timeline

  1. 1990s

    Terry Davis works as a programmer, including on real-time systems, after studying electrical engineering and computer science.

  2. 2003

    Following a mental health crisis, Terry begins what he describes as a directive to build an operating system, later framed as a "temple" commanded by God.

  3. 2003–2010

    Solo development of the kernel, the HolyC language and its compiler/interpreter, a custom filesystem, and a 640x480, 16-color graphics stack.

  4. 2013

    TempleOS reaches a self-described "complete" 64-bit release after roughly a decade of work, alongside a large body of related writing.

  5. 2010s

    Terry livestreams programming sessions, drawing a large online audience, some respectful and some exploitative of his visible mental illness.

  6. 2018

    Terry Davis dies after being struck by a train, following years of struggle with schizophrenia and periods of homelessness.
